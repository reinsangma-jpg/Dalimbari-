01
Overview
Introduction

As part of my field study, I visited Dalimbari Gaon, located in the West Karbi Anglong district of Assam, under the Amri Development Block. The village is known for its rural setting, greenery, and a landscape shaped by hills, forest patches, streams/rivers, and low-lying areas.

Dalimbari is mainly inhabited by the Karbi community and has a strong community life. Along with the Karbi majority, other communities and religions also live here, making the village socially diverse. The daily life of villagers is closely linked with agriculture, local markets, small wage work, and nearby plantation-related activities.

Approach
Methodology

04
Physical Setting
Location & Geography

Dalimbari Gaon lies within West Karbi Anglong, a district characterized by hills and valleys. The village is connected to nearby areas through rural roads, but connectivity becomes difficult during monsoon due to weak and broken stretches.

A notable regional point is the Basundari border area, which marks the boundary between West Karbi Anglong and Nagaon district.

🗺️
Plate 2 — Hand-drawn location map: Assam → West Karbi Anglong → Dalimbari

Relief and Terrain

The village has a mixed terrain with distinct zones:

  • Lower zones: market area, cultivation land, and active movement of people.
  • Uphill/hillside zones: habitations extend towards upper areas (Phukrap side) with slopes and forest patches.

Sub-tropical climate characteristics: Heavy monsoon rainfall makes roads muddy and broken; mild winters; warm summers with increased water demand.

Drainage & Water Bodies

Water bodies are among the most important natural features of Dalimbari. The Chinanadi stream supports daily needs and agriculture in nearby areas. The Naudubi River is another important natural resource for agriculture and water availability in surrounding zones.

Community Layout
Settlement Pattern

Dalimbari is not one single compact settlement — it spreads across several distinct localities and habitations, each with its own character.

Housing

Traditional rural house types are common — bamboo/wood construction is visible, often with tin roofs. Houses are spaced with small yards and household plants. Settlement becomes more scattered towards upper slope areas.

Heritage & Faith
Culture, Religion & Worship

Dalimbari reflects Karbi cultural identity along with broader Assamese rural influence. Traditional social practices remain important and community participation is strong.

Major Festivals

  • Rongker — Community worship festival
  • Chomangkan — Traditional death ceremony/festival

The village is majority Hindu, mainly among the Karbi community. Christian and Muslim families also reside here alongside Bengali community presence.

07
Livelihoods
Occupation & Economy

The village economy is mainly based on primary activities and local services. Economic condition is generally low to moderate.

Main Occupations

  • Agriculture — paddy, vegetables, local crops (main occupation)
  • Daily wage labour — seasonal and regular
  • Small shops and market-based work — bazar activities
  • Animal husbandry — household-level keeping

Agriculture & Cropping

Agriculture depends heavily on rainfall. Paddy cultivation in low-lying plots, vegetables and seasonal crops in small fields, and in some hillside zones, jhum/shifting cultivation is also practiced.

Tea Gardens

Dalimbari has two tea garden areas providing important local wage opportunities:

  • Tea garden near the Chinanadi river side
  • Tea garden near Dalimbari Bill (Beel)

09
Issues Identified
Problems & Challenges

🛣️ Poor Road Condition

Weak and broken stretches throughout the village. Situation worsens during monsoon, affecting school, market, and emergency travel.

🏥 Limited Healthcare

Health Care Sub-Centre exists but serious cases must travel far. Poor roads delay emergency referrals.

💧 Seasonal Water Issues

Two supply facilities exist but dry-season shortage and maintenance gaps remain concerns.

📱 Weak Connectivity

Mobile network weak near hillside zones. Limited internet affects students and online service access.

💼 Limited Employment

Dependence on farming, tea garden, and wage labour. Fewer stable income options in off-season.

📖 Education Gaps

Schools lack digital facilities and libraries. Monsoon disrupts student attendance due to road conditions.

Even with transport availability, the road condition is poor, weak, and broken in several parts. During monsoon, roads become worse — affecting school travel, market supply, and hospital referral during emergencies.

10
Recommendations
Suggestions
1
Immediate improvement of roads with stronger materials and proper side-drainage systems.
2
Regular maintenance of both water supply facilities; awareness campaigns about safe water practices.
3
Strengthen the Health Care Sub-Centre — medicine availability, regular staff presence, and basic equipment.
4
Upgrade school facilities: computers, library materials, playground and sports support infrastructure.
5
Promote skill training and local employment programs especially for youth to reduce seasonal unemployment.
6
Improve communication networks and internet access for education and digital services.
7
Protect slope areas from erosion through plantation drives and proper drainage management.
11
What I Learned
Personal Reflection

This field visit helped me understand the real situation of a rural village beyond textbooks. Dalimbari is rich in nature and culture, and people work hard with strong unity.

I learned that development must be balanced — roads, education, water, health, and employment all need to improve together for real progress. No single change is enough; it takes a whole-village approach.

12
Final Summary
Conclusion

Dalimbari Gaon in West Karbi Anglong is a developing rural village with strong Karbi cultural roots, diverse communities, and important natural surroundings. The village has key facilities — a daily market, playground, two water supply facilities, a health care sub-centre, public transport, eight schools, two tea garden areas, and five places of worship.

However, poor road conditions and limited healthcare capacity remain the most pressing challenges. With proper planning, road improvement, facility maintenance, and support for education and employment, Dalimbari can develop further while preserving its traditions and environment.
